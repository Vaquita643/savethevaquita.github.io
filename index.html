Vaquita Facts

  • Status: Critically Endangered
  • Population: ~20 individuals
  • Height: 5 feet
  • Weight: 120 lbs (54.4 kg)
  • Habitat: Marine (Gulf of California)

What's happening

Vaquitas and other aquatic animals are at risk due to illegal gillnet fishing in protected marine areas. These animals are not the target of the fishing campaigns, yet often find themselves tangled up in nets.

We believe that protected species should be better protected from illegal fishing, and therefore have prepared this informational website to teach people about The Plight of the Vaquita, and what we can do to prevent other aquatic wildlife from following a similar path. See below for a list of other endangered animals that need your support.

Aquatic Wildlife at risk

Blue Whale

The blue whale is largest animal on the planet, weighing as much as 200 tons. Like other large whales, blue whales are threatened by environmental change including habitat loss and toxics. They can also be harmed by ship strikes and by becoming entangled in fishing gear.

Whale Shark

They are the largest fish in the sea known to grow to 18 metres long and weigh up to 20 tons. They are currently endangered as they are highly valued on the international market, with the demand for their meat, fins and oil remains a threat to the species.

Click for more information
Hawksbill Turtle

They are one of the smaller sea turtles that are found mainly throughout the world’s tropical oceans. They are currently critically endangered as they are mostly threatened by wildlife trade, with there being a large amount of illegal trade in Hawksbill shell and products.

Click for more information
Sea Otter

This aquatic member of the weasel family is found along the coasts of the Pacific Ocean in North America and Asia. With the otter population, humans are the biggest threat with shooting and entrapment in fishing traps, but oil spills and loss of kelp forest are also causing sea otters to be endangered.

Hector's Dolphin

These dolphins are the smallest and rarest marine dolphins in the world, with an estimated population of only 55. They are only found along the western shored of New Zealand's North Island. As they live close to shore, this is problem for the dolphins due to bycatch, becoming tangled in trawl nets, is the biggest threat they face.

Click for more information
Loggerhead Turtle

Loggerhead Turtles are named for their large heads and are the most common turtle in the Mediterranean. Compared to other sea turtles, they are less likely hunted for their meat or shell. However, their current extinction risk is 'vulnerable' due to the threat of bycatch, being accidentally caught on longline hooks and in fishing gillnets.
